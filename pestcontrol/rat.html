Preventing Rat Infestations

The most effective method for preventing rat infestations is to provide as little shelter and storage as possible, as this is likely to entice rats and other rodents. It is important to plug up as many openings and crevices as you can find on the exterior of the house where rats could gain access. It is recommended that the following guidelines be adhered to in order to help prevent rats from entering your home in search of the food and shelter it offers: If it is at all possible, you should remove any piles of debris, stones, bricks, or other materials that are located close to the foundation of your home. These piles serve as harborages for rodents. Patch up any holes or cracks that are more than a quarter of an inch wide. If you want to prevent rodents from entering your home through large holes or cracks, you should first stuff them with steel wool or wire mesh before sealing the openings with caulk or foam.
