What Are the Signs of a Spider Infestation?

Spiders are frequently found in the dark, in the corners of rooms or houses, in buildings, and both inside and outside your home. Check out the list below for some warning signs of a spider infestation.

  • Spider webs vary in size and shape depending on the species, with some being orb-shaped and others funnel-shaped.
  • Spiders feed on other insects and prey on flies, woodlice, ants, and other spiders, so they thrive in environments where other insects are abundant.
  • Few spider species live in holes rather than webs, while others are free-roaming and seek shelter in cracks.
  • Some spiders are attracted to moist environments. Examine the walls, basements, sheds, and other damp areas in your home.
  • Spider eggs are laid in silken sacs with 100 eggs each, which are carried by the female or hidden in the web.
  • Other species can be found in attics, ceiling-to-wall junctions, closets, and storage boxes, among other places.
  • The presence of the sacs fixed indoors indicates that more spiders will be present soon.

How to Get Rid of a Spider Infestation

Spiders are among the most incredible creatures on the planet, and we can thank them for keeping pests like mosquitoes and flies at bay. However, this does not imply that you should allow them to live in your home’s corners, windows, or behind your bathroom door. Here are some helpful hints for keeping trespassers away from your property.

  • Vacuum frequently, both high and low, particularly in sheltered areas such as the backs of cupboards, beneath worktops, or under or behind large furniture.
  • On a regular basis, remove any visible webs.
  • Fill gaps in walls, around pipework, under doors, and in dark areas to deter entry.
  • Get rid of any places on your property where animals might be able to hide, like garden bags, compost piles, firewood piles, and general clutter.
  • To get rid of all insects, use lighting that is less appealing to the insects (flies) that spiders eat.
