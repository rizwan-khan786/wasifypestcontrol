Mosquitoes are true flies in the order Diptera. Mosquito wings, like all true flies, have scales. Female mosquito mouthparts form a long, piercing, sucking proboscis. Females have feathery antennae and mouthparts that are not suitable for piercing skin. The primary food of a mosquito is nectar or a similar sugar source. Mosquito control can be divided into two categories: individual and public. It is usually done in accordance with the Integrated Mosquito Control Management (IMM) concept. IMM is based on ecological, economic, and social criteria, and it combines methods from different fields to make practical and effective pest management plans that protect public health and the environment and make people’s lives better.

Mosquito Control

IMM strategies are used in conjunction with insecticides. These include source reduction, which includes both physical control (digging ditches and ponds in the target marsh) and biological control (placing live mosquito fish (Gambusia) in the ditches and ponds to eat mosquito larvae). Other non-chemical mosquito larvae control methods include invertebrate predators, parasites, and diseases. Various agencies have used adult mosquito biological control using birds, bats, dragonflies, and frogs. But the evidence is anecdotal, and there is no study that shows bats, purple martins, or other predators eat enough adult mosquitos to be effective control agents.

Keeping mosquitoes at bay is a long-term process, and we need your help to maintain basic best practices to avoid mosquito breeding.

To reduce mosquito breeding, follow the steps below:

  • Get rid of old tires, buckets, pots, and other containers on your property that collect rainwater or morning dew.
  • Fill in tree-rot holes that are holding water.
  • Tires with water in their crevices should be discarded.
  • Empty still water from decorative containers on your property, such as birdfeeders, stands, and pots, at least once per week.
  • Clean clogged roof gutters, especially if leaves from nearby trees have clogged the drain.
  • Clean and chlorinate inactive swimming pools. A swimming pool that has been left unclean for a month can produce swarms of mosquitoes that can infest an entire neighborhood.
